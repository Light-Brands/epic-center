# Mexico Regulatory Requirements for Transformational Epicenter

> *Comprehensive guide to licenses, permits, and regulatory compliance for operating a medical wellness facility in Mexico*

---

## Executive Summary

Operating a medical wellness facility in Mexico's Quintana Roo region requires navigating multiple regulatory bodies at federal, state, and municipal levels. This document outlines all required permits, licenses, and compliance requirements, with particular emphasis on regenerative medicine and plant medicine operations.

**Key Regulatory Bodies:**
- **COFEPRIS** (Federal Commission for Protection against Sanitary Risks) - Primary health regulatory authority
- **SSA** (Secretaría de Salud) - Ministry of Health
- **SEDETUR** (Secretaría de Turismo) - Tourism Ministry
- **Municipal authorities** - Local permits and zoning

## 1. Medical Facility Licenses

### 1.1 Sanitary License (Licencia Sanitaria)

**Issuing Authority:** COFEPRIS / State Health Authority

**Description:** Required for any establishment providing medical services. This is the foundational license for operating as a healthcare facility.

**Requirements:**
- Registered healthcare facility with SSA
- Designated responsible medical director (Médico Responsable)
- Facility inspection and approval
- Compliance with NOM-005-SSA3-2010 (healthcare facility standards)
- Emergency protocols documented
- Medical waste management plan

**Documentation Required:**
- [ ] Facility floor plans and specifications
- [ ] Medical director credentials and registration
- [ ] Equipment inventory and certifications
- [ ] Emergency response protocols
- [ ] Staff credentials and certifications
- [ ] Insurance documentation

**Timeline:** 3-6 months from application
**Cost Estimate:** $5,000-$15,000 USD (including legal fees)

### 1.2 Medical Director Registration

**Issuing Authority:** State Health Authority / COFEPRIS

**Requirements:**
- Valid Mexican medical license (cédula profesional)
- Specialty certification if applicable
- Clean professional record
- Physical presence requirement

**For Foreign Physicians:**
- Credential validation through SEP (Secretaría de Educación Pública)
- CONACEM specialty certification (if applicable)
- Immigration status permitting professional work

### 1.3 Healthcare Facility Classification

**Classification Options:**

| Type | Description | Applicable? |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| Consultorio | Basic medical office | No - too limited |
| Clínica | Outpatient clinic with procedures | Possible |
| Hospital | Inpatient facility with overnight stays | Yes - recommended |
| Centro de Rehabilitación | Rehabilitation center | Possible addition |

**Recommendation:** Register as a **Hospital** or **Clínica con Internamiento** (clinic with hospitalization) to allow:
- Overnight patient stays
- IV therapy administration
- Medical monitoring
- Emergency response capability

### 1.4 Controlled Substances License

**Issuing Authority:** COFEPRIS

**Required for:**
- Ketamine administration
- Certain sedatives
- Emergency medications
- Pain management protocols

**Requirements:**
- Designated controlled substances manager
- Secure storage facilities
- Inventory tracking systems
- Regular reporting to COFEPRIS
- DEA-equivalent oversight protocols

## 2. Regenerative Medicine Permits

### 2.1 COFEPRIS Regenerative Medicine Authorization

**This is a CRITICAL permit for Transformational Epicenter's bio-optimization offerings.**

**Issuing Authority:** COFEPRIS - Comisión de Control y Fomento Sanitario

**Scope Covers:**
- Stem cell therapies
- PRP (Platelet-Rich Plasma) treatments
- Exosome therapies
- NAD+ infusions
- Cell-based regenerative treatments

**Requirements:**

**Facility Requirements:**
- [ ] Clean room/sterile processing area (Class 10,000 or better)
- [ ] Proper air filtration (HEPA)
- [ ] Temperature-controlled storage
- [ ] Validated sterilization equipment
- [ ] Backup power systems

**Personnel Requirements:**
- [ ] Qualified medical director with regenerative medicine training
- [ ] Laboratory technicians with cell therapy certification
- [ ] Nursing staff trained in biologics administration

**Documentation Requirements:**
- [ ] Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs) for all treatments
- [ ] Quality control protocols
- [ ] Adverse event reporting procedures
- [ ] Patient consent forms approved by ethics committee
- [ ] Treatment protocols with scientific backing
- [ ] Source documentation for biological materials

**Process:**
1. Pre-application consultation with COFEPRIS
2. Facility inspection scheduling
3. Documentation submission
4. On-site inspection
5. Conditional approval / deficiency correction
6. Final authorization

**Timeline:** 6-12 months
**Cost Estimate:** $20,000-$50,000 USD (facility upgrades + legal/consulting fees)

### 2.2 Cell Therapy Specific Requirements

**For Stem Cell Treatments:**
- Cell sourcing documentation (autologous vs. allogeneic)
- Processing protocols
- Quality testing requirements
- Storage and handling procedures
- Patient selection criteria

**For Exosome/NAD+ Therapies:**
- Product source verification
- Import permits (if sourced internationally)
- Storage requirements
- Administration protocols

### 2.3 Current Regulatory Landscape (2025-2026)

Mexico's regenerative medicine framework is evolving. Key considerations:

- **NOM-260-SSA1-2014**: Stem cell therapy standards (in development)
- **Autologous treatments**: Generally permitted with proper facility licensing
- **Allogeneic treatments**: Stricter oversight, may require clinical trial framework
- **Experimental status**: Some treatments may need to operate under "experimental protocol" designation

**Strategic Recommendation:** Work with a regulatory consultant who has direct COFEPRIS relationships to navigate evolving requirements.

## 3. Plant Medicine Legal Framework

### 3.1 Ibogaine Status in Mexico

**Legal Status:** Unscheduled / Legal

Ibogaine is not listed in Mexico's controlled substances schedules, making it legal to possess, administer, and use in therapeutic settings.

**Operational Requirements:**
- Medical oversight during administration
- Cardiac monitoring capability
- Emergency response protocols
- No specific COFEPRIS permit required for the substance itself
- Facility must have appropriate medical licensing (see Section 1)

### 3.2 Psilocybin Status

**Legal Status:** Decriminalized for traditional/ceremonial use

**Key Considerations:**
- Traditional/indigenous ceremonial use is protected
- Commercial therapeutic use exists in legal grey area
- No explicit prohibition on therapeutic administration
- Recommended: Partner with indigenous practitioners for ceremonial framework

### 3.3 Other Plant Medicines

| Substance | Legal Status | Notes |
|-----------|--------------|-------|
| Ayahuasca (DMT) | Controlled - DMT scheduled | Not recommended for inclusion |
| 5-MeO-DMT | Grey area | Proceed with caution |
| Kambo | Unregulated | Legal to administer |
| San Pedro | Unregulated | Legal for ceremonial use |

### 3.4 Recommended Operational Framework

To minimize regulatory risk while maximizing therapeutic benefit:

1. **Primary Focus:** Ibogaine (clear legal status)
2. **Secondary:** Psilocybin within ceremonial/traditional framework
3. **Avoid:** DMT-containing substances until regulatory clarity improves
4. **Medical Integration:** All plant medicine work conducted under medical supervision
5. **Documentation:** Comprehensive medical records, consent forms, screening protocols

## 4. Hospitality & Tourism Permits

### 4.1 Tourism Registry (Registro Nacional de Turismo)

**Issuing Authority:** SEDETUR (State) / SECTUR (Federal)

**Requirements:**
- Business registration
- Facility classification
- Safety compliance
- Accessibility standards

### 4.2 Hotel/Lodging License

**Issuing Authority:** Municipal government

**Requirements:**
- Zoning compliance
- Building permits
- Fire safety certification
- Health inspection (separate from medical)
- Accessibility compliance

**Classification:** Likely "Boutique Hotel" or "Wellness Resort" category

### 4.3 Food Service License (Licencia de Funcionamiento - Alimentos)

**Issuing Authority:** COFEPRIS / Municipal health authority

**Requirements:**
- Kitchen inspection
- Food handler certifications
- HACCP compliance
- Water quality testing
- Pest control documentation

### 4.4 Alcohol License (if applicable)

**Issuing Authority:** State alcohol control board

**Note:** Consider whether alcohol service aligns with wellness mission. May choose to operate alcohol-free.

## 5. Employment & Labor Requirements

### 5.1 Employer Registration

**Issuing Authority:** IMSS (Instituto Mexicano del Seguro Social)

**Requirements:**
- Employer registration number
- Employee enrollment in social security
- Workplace safety compliance
- Workers' compensation coverage

### 5.2 Foreign Worker Permits

**For international staff (physicians, specialists):**

| Permit Type | Duration | Requirements |
|-------------|----------|--------------|
| FM3 (Temporary Resident - Work) | 1-4 years | Job offer, qualifications |
| FM2 (Permanent Resident) | Indefinite | 4 years FM3 or other criteria |
| Professional Services Visa | Varies | Specific professional engagement |

**Process:**
1. Job offer from Mexican entity
2. INM (Immigration) application
3. Credential validation (if professional)
4. Work permit issuance

### 5.3 Professional Licensing for Foreign Healthcare Workers

**Physicians:**
- SEP credential validation
- Cédula profesional issuance
- CONACEM specialty certification
- State medical board registration

**Timeline:** 6-12 months for full credential validation

**Alternative:** Foreign physicians can work under supervision of Mexican-licensed physician or as consultants (limited scope)

### 5.4 Labor Law Compliance

**Key Requirements:**
- Written employment contracts
- Minimum wage compliance
- Profit sharing (PTU) - 10% of profits to employees
- Vacation, bonus (aguinaldo), and severance compliance
- Working hours regulations

## 6. Environmental Permits

### 6.1 Environmental Impact Assessment (MIA)

**Issuing Authority:** SEMARNAT (Federal) / State environmental agency

**Required if:**
- New construction
- Significant renovation
- Coastal zone location
- Protected area proximity

**Quintana Roo Specific:**
- Coastal development restrictions
- Mangrove protection requirements
- Cenote/aquifer protection
- Wildlife corridor considerations

### 6.2 Water & Wastewater

**Requirements:**
- Water rights (CONAGUA)
- Wastewater treatment system
- Discharge permits
- Water quality monitoring

### 6.3 Medical Waste Management

**Issuing Authority:** SEMARNAT / COFEPRIS

**Requirements:**
- RPBI (Residuos Peligrosos Biológico-Infecciosos) management plan
- Licensed waste disposal contractor
- Proper storage and handling
- Transportation documentation
- Annual reporting

## 7. Corporate Structure Requirements

### 7.1 Mexican Entity Formation

**Recommended Structure:** Sociedad Anónima de Capital Variable (S.A. de C.V.)

**Requirements:**
- Minimum 2 shareholders
- Registered agent in Mexico
- Notarized articles of incorporation
- Tax registration (RFC)
- Bank account establishment

**Foreign Ownership Considerations:**
- Healthcare sector: Generally permits 100% foreign ownership
- Real estate in restricted zone (coastal): Requires fideicomiso (bank trust)
- Review specific sector restrictions

### 7.2 Required Registrations

| Registration | Authority | Purpose |
|--------------|-----------|---------|
| RFC (Tax ID) | SAT | Tax compliance |
| IMSS | IMSS | Social security |
| INFONAVIT | INFONAVIT | Housing fund |
| State Tax | State authority | Local taxes |

### 7.3 Fideicomiso (Bank Trust) for Real Estate

**Required for:** Property ownership within 50km of coast or 100km of border

**Structure:**
- Mexican bank holds title as trustee
- Foreign entity is beneficiary
- 50-year renewable term
- Full usage and transfer rights

**Cost:** $2,000-$5,000 USD setup + annual fees (~$1,000-2,000)
