## 3. Assignment

### 3.1 Irrevocable Assignment

The Assignor hereby irrevocably assigns, transfers, conveys, and delivers to the Assignee, without reservation, condition, or limitation, 100% of the Assignor's right, title, and interest in and to the Assigned PPAs, including without limitation:

(a) **Profit distributions** — all quarterly profit distributions under both the LBS PPA and the LBC PPA, calculated per the 14 Bulletproof Principles;

(b) **Exit participation** — all rights to participate in exit proceeds upon sale, merger, consolidation, IPO, liquidation, or wind-down of LBS or LBC;

(c) **Governance rights** — all board seats, observer rights, veto rights, consent rights, and voting rights granted under the Assigned PPAs;

(d) **Accumulation account** — all accrued and future balances in the accumulation accounts maintained under each PPA;

(e) **Anti-dilution rights** — all pre-emptive rights, anti-dilution protections, and consent rights regarding new equity or PPA issuances;

(f) **Audit and information rights** — all rights to receive financial statements, conduct audits, and access books and records of LBS and LBC;

(g) **Specific performance and enforcement** — all rights to seek specific performance, injunctive relief, acceleration, and damages under the Assigned PPAs;

(h) **Most-favored-nation rights** — all rights to receive improved terms granted to any other PPA holder;

(i) **All ancillary rights** — every other right, benefit, privilege, or interest arising under, connected to, or derived from the Assigned PPAs, whether now existing or hereafter arising.

### 3.2 Completeness

The assignment is total and complete. No right, benefit, privilege, or interest under the Assigned PPAs is retained by the Assignor. From the Effective Date forward, the Church stands in the Assignor's place for all purposes under both PPAs.

## 4. Irrevocability

### 4.1 Permanent and Unconditional

This assignment is **irrevocable**. The Assignor permanently and unconditionally waives all right to:

(a) Reclaim any portion of the Assigned PPAs;

(b) Modify, alter, or redirect any distribution, right, or interest under the Assigned PPAs;

(c) Revoke, rescind, or cancel this Assignment Agreement;

(d) Assert any claim, lien, or interest in the Assigned PPAs after the Effective Date;

(e) Instruct LBS, LBC, S.A., or PIF to pay any distribution or exit proceeds to anyone other than the Church.

### 4.2 No Condition Subsequent

No event, circumstance, or change in condition shall cause this assignment to lapse, terminate, or revert. The Assignor cannot create a condition subsequent by any act or omission.

### 4.3 Assignment of Income Doctrine Defense

The irrevocability of this assignment is specifically designed to defeat any challenge under the assignment of income doctrine (Lucas v. Earl, 281 U.S. 111 (1930)). Because the Assignor retains zero economic interest, zero governance rights, and zero ability to redirect distributions, the Assignor does not "own" the income for federal tax purposes. The Church is the economic owner from the Effective Date forward.

## 5. No Retained Interest

### 5.1 Zero Interest

After the Effective Date, the Assignor retains **zero** economic or governance interest in the Assigned PPAs, LBS, or LBC. Specifically:

(a) The Assignor has no right to receive any distribution from LBS or LBC;

(b) The Assignor has no right to participate in any exit event;

(c) The Assignor has no right to any accumulation account balance;

(d) The Assignor has no right to appoint a board representative, exercise a veto, or vote on any matter under the Assigned PPAs;

(e) The Assignor has no right to access the books, records, or financial statements of LBS or LBC through the Assigned PPAs.

### 5.2 Church as Sole Holder

From the Effective Date, the Church is the sole and exclusive holder of the Assigned PPAs and exercises all rights thereunder through its Spiritual Council or its designees.

## 9. Survival

### 9.1 Survival Events

This assignment survives each of the following events without any effect on its validity, enforceability, or scope:

(a) **Death of the Assignor** — the Church retains the Assigned PPAs in perpetuity;

(b) **Incapacity of the Assignor** — the Church's rights are unaffected;

(c) **Departure of the Assignor from the Church** — voluntary resignation, removal for cause, or any other cessation of the Assignor's service as Senior Pastor;

(d) **Change in relationship with the Church** — any change in the Assignor's title, role, compensation, or status within the Church;

(e) **Change in marital status** — divorce, separation, remarriage, or any other change in the Assignor's personal or domestic circumstances;

(f) **Bankruptcy or insolvency of the Assignor** — the Assigned PPAs are not property of the Assignor's estate;

(g) **Dissolution or restructuring of any entity** — per the Survival principles (Principle 12) of the underlying PPAs;

(h) **Any challenge by a third party** — including creditors, former spouses, heirs, or governmental authorities.

### 9.2 Perpetual Duration

This assignment has no expiration date, sunset clause, or term limitation. It is perpetual.

## Counsel Review Notes

1. **Gift tax on assignment:** What is the fair market value of the Assigned PPAs at the date of transfer? If the PPAs have significant present value (based on projected distributions and exit), gift tax may apply. Can the assignment be structured as a present-interest gift to qualify for the annual exclusion, or does the nature of PPA distributions make it a future-interest gift? Counsel to advise on Form 709 filing requirements.

2. **Qualified appraisal:** If the aggregate value of the Assigned PPAs exceeds $5,000, Form 8283 and a qualified appraisal may be required to substantiate a charitable contribution deduction. Should Daniel claim the deduction, or is it cleaner to forgo the deduction and avoid the valuation dispute entirely?

3. **Assignment of income doctrine:** The irrevocability and completeness of this assignment are designed to defeat an assignment of income challenge. Counsel to confirm that (a) the irrevocable nature of the assignment, (b) the absence of any retained interest, and (c) the entity-level consents are sufficient to shift the income to the Church for federal tax purposes.

4. **State law formalities:** Does the state of the Church's formation require specific formalities for the acceptance of donated contract rights (board resolution, written acceptance, recording)? Is the Spiritual Council resolution sufficient?

5. **Bankruptcy remoteness:** If Daniel files for bankruptcy after the assignment, could a trustee challenge the assignment as a fraudulent transfer under state or federal law? The irrevocable, completed nature of the gift should defeat this, but counsel should confirm the applicable lookback periods and defenses.

6. **Multi-jurisdiction enforcement:** This assignment involves US (Church), UAE (LBS), Cook Islands (LBC), and Panama (S.A., PIF). Counsel to confirm that the assignment is enforceable in all four jurisdictions and that each entity's governing law permits recognition of the Church as PPA holder.

7. **Vesting interaction:** The LBS PPA (D03) includes a 4-year vesting schedule. Does this assignment transfer vested and unvested portions? Can the Church be subject to vesting, or does assignment to an institutional holder accelerate vesting? Counsel to reconcile.

8. **PIF consent authority:** Does the PIF's founding document permit the Foundation Council to consent to PPA assignments? Or must the Protector or a designated beneficiary also consent?
