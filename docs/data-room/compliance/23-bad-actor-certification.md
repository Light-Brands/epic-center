## 1. Purpose & Legal Basis

### 1.1 Overview

Rule 506(d) of Regulation D (17 CFR 230.506(d)) requires issuers to conduct a "bad actor" inquiry before relying on the Rule 506 exemption from registration. If any "covered person" has experienced a "disqualifying event" as defined in Rule 506(d)(1), the issuer is disqualified from using the Rule 506 exemption unless an exception applies.

### 1.2 Applicability

This certification applies to the offering of [To be determined -- LLC Membership Interests / Preferred Units / SAFE] by Transformational Epicenter Holdings, LLC (the "Company") under Rule 506(b) or 506(c) [Pending counsel determination] of Regulation D, and to any future Regulation D offerings by the Company.

### 1.3 Regulatory References

- **Securities Act of 1933, as amended** -- Section 4(a)(2)
- **Regulation D** -- Rules 501-506 (17 CFR 230.501-506)
- **Rule 506(d)** -- Disqualification of felons and other "bad actors"
- **Rule 506(e)** -- Disclosure of prior "bad actor" events
- **SEC Release No. 33-9414** (July 10, 2013) -- Adopting release for Rule 506(d)

## 3. Disqualifying Events

### 3.1 Categories of Disqualifying Events

Under Rule 506(d)(1), the following constitute disqualifying events:

**(A) Criminal Convictions**
- Felony or misdemeanor conviction in connection with the purchase or sale of any security
- Felony or misdemeanor conviction involving making any untrue statement of material fact or omitting a material fact
- Felony or misdemeanor in connection with the conduct of the business of an underwriter, broker, dealer, municipal securities dealer, investment adviser, or paid solicitor of purchasers of securities
- **Lookback period:** 10 years (5 years for the issuer and its predecessors/affiliates)

**(B) Court Injunctions and Restraining Orders**
- Court orders restraining or enjoining a person from engaging in or continuing any conduct or practice in connection with securities activities, underwriting, or broker-dealer/investment adviser conduct
- **Lookback period:** 5 years

**(C) Final Orders from Specified Regulators**
- Final orders from state securities, insurance, banking, savings, or credit union regulators; federal banking agencies; the CFTC; or the National Credit Union Administration that:
  - Bar the person from association with a regulated entity, or engaging in securities/insurance/banking business
  - Constitute a final order based on violations of laws prohibiting fraudulent, manipulative, or deceptive conduct
- **Lookback period:** 10 years

**(D) SEC Disciplinary Orders**
- SEC disciplinary orders under Section 15(b) or 15B(c) of the Exchange Act, or Section 203(e) or (f) of the Investment Advisers Act suspending or revoking registration, or placing limitations on activities, functions, or operations
- **Lookback period:** No time limit

**(E) SEC Cease-and-Desist Orders**
- SEC cease-and-desist orders under Section 8A of the Securities Act, Section 21C of the Exchange Act, Section 9(f) of the Investment Company Act, or Section 203(k) of the Investment Advisers Act involving scienter-based anti-fraud provisions
- **Lookback period:** No time limit

**(F) SEC Stop Orders and Suspension of Reg A Exemption**
- SEC stop orders or orders suspending the Regulation A exemption
- **Lookback period:** 5 years

**(G) USPS False Representation Orders**
- United States Postal Service false representation orders
- **Lookback period:** 5 years

**(H) SRO Disciplinary Actions**
- Expulsion or suspension from membership in, or association with, any SRO; or barred or suspended from being associated with an SRO member
- **Lookback period:** No time limit (for expulsion/bar); 10 years (for suspension)

### 3.2 Exceptions

Disqualification does not apply if:

1. **Pre-September 23, 2013 events** -- Events occurring before the effective date of Rule 506(d) are not disqualifying but must be disclosed under Rule 506(e)
2. **Court or regulatory determination** -- The court or regulatory authority that entered the order advises in writing that disqualification should not arise
3. **SEC waiver** -- The SEC grants a waiver upon a showing of good cause

## 4. Certification Requirements

### 4.1 Timing

- **Initial certification:** Required before the first sale of securities in any Regulation D offering
- **Updated certification:** Required upon any change in covered persons or upon becoming aware of any disqualifying event
- **Annual re-certification:** Required annually for all covered persons for the duration of any ongoing offering

### 4.2 Process

1. **Distribution:** The Company distributes this questionnaire to all identified covered persons
2. **Completion:** Each covered person completes, signs, and returns the certification
3. **Review:** [To be designated] (legal counsel) reviews all completed certifications
4. **Determination:** Counsel provides written determination of compliance or non-compliance
5. **Filing:** Completed certifications are maintained in the Company's compliance files
6. **Remediation:** If any disqualifying event is identified, the Company follows the remediation procedures in Section 8

### 4.3 Verification

The Company will conduct reasonable factual inquiry to verify certifications, which may include:

- Background checks through licensed investigation firms
- FINRA BrokerCheck verification
- SEC EDGAR and IAPD database searches
- State securities regulator database searches
- Court records searches in relevant jurisdictions

## 8. Remediation Procedures

### 8.1 If a Disqualifying Event Is Identified

1. **Immediate notification** to CEO and legal counsel
2. **Suspend offering** until determination is made
3. **Legal analysis** by counsel to determine:
   - Whether the event is within the lookback period
   - Whether an exception applies (pre-2013, regulatory determination, or SEC waiver)
   - Whether the covered person can be removed from covered person status
4. **If no exception applies:**
   - Pursue SEC waiver under Rule 506(d)(2)(ii)
   - Remove the individual from covered person status if feasible
   - If the individual cannot be removed and no waiver is available, the Company must consider alternative exemptions for the offering
5. **Documentation** of all steps taken and determinations made
6. **Investor notification** if required under securities laws

### 8.2 Rule 506(e) Disclosure

If a disqualifying event occurred before September 23, 2013, or is otherwise subject to disclosure rather than disqualification, the Company must:

1. Furnish a written description of the event to each purchaser
2. Provide disclosure a reasonable time prior to the sale
3. Maintain records of the disclosure and purchaser acknowledgment
