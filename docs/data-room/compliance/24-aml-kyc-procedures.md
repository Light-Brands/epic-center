## IMPORTANT NOTICE

**THIS DOCUMENT ESTABLISHES THE ANTI-MONEY LAUNDERING AND KNOW YOUR CUSTOMER PROCEDURES** for Transformational Epicenter Holdings, LLC (the "Company") in connection with its securities offerings under Regulation D of the Securities Act of 1933, as amended. While the Company is not a "financial institution" subject to the Bank Secrecy Act, the Company voluntarily adopts these procedures as a best practice and to meet obligations under applicable securities laws, broker-dealer agreements, and the requirements of the Company's registered broker-dealer (to be designated).

**NOTE ON INTERNATIONAL OPERATIONS:** The Company operates a medical wellness and ibogaine therapy center in Tulum, Quintana Roo, Mexico. AML/KYC procedures must account for both U.S. securities regulations and potential cross-border considerations arising from Mexico-based operations, including compliance with Mexico's Federal Law for the Prevention and Identification of Operations with Resources of Illicit Origin (LFPIORPI) to the extent applicable.

## 1. Program Overview

### 1.1 Purpose

This AML/KYC Program establishes policies and procedures designed to:

- Detect and prevent money laundering, terrorist financing, and other illicit financial activities
- Ensure compliance with the USA PATRIOT Act, Bank Secrecy Act, and related regulations
- Meet the requirements of the Company's broker-dealer [To be designated]
- Satisfy "reasonable steps" verification requirements under Rule 506(b) or 506(c) [Pending counsel determination] of Regulation D
- Protect the Company and its investors from association with sanctioned or prohibited persons
- Address cross-border compliance considerations arising from the Company's Mexico-based operations

### 1.2 Regulatory Framework

| Regulation | Applicability |
|------------|--------------|
| **Bank Secrecy Act (BSA)** | Framework for AML compliance |
| **USA PATRIOT Act, Title III** | Enhanced due diligence requirements |
| **OFAC Sanctions Programs** | Prohibited persons screening |
| **Rule 506(b) / 506(c)** | Accredited investor verification [Pending counsel determination] |
| **FinCEN Customer Due Diligence Rule** | Beneficial ownership identification |
| **FINRA Rules 3310, 3110** | Via [To be designated] broker-dealer compliance |
| **Mexico LFPIORPI** | Anti-money laundering framework for Mexico operations (to the extent applicable) |

### 1.3 Scope

This program applies to:

- All prospective investors in the Company's Reg D offering
- All subscriptions, whether received directly or through the Company's broker-dealer [To be designated]
- All officers, employees, and agents of the Company involved in fundraising activities
- All third-party service providers involved in investor onboarding

| Email address | Yes | Self-reported |

### 3.2 Required Information -- Entity Investors

| Information | Required | Source |
|-------------|----------|--------|

| Jurisdiction of formation | Yes | Formation documents |

| Control person(s) | Yes | FinCEN CDD Rule |

### 3.3 Beneficial Ownership Requirements

Under the FinCEN Customer Due Diligence Rule, the Company must identify and verify:

- **All beneficial owners** holding 25% or more equity interest in the entity investor
- **At least one control person** (individual with significant management responsibility)

For each beneficial owner and control person, collect the same information required for individual investors (Section 3.1).

### 3.4 Verification Procedures

| Method | Application |
|--------|-------------|
| **Documentary verification** | Government-issued ID with photo (primary method) |
| **Non-documentary verification** | Third-party database check (TransUnion, LexisNexis, or equivalent) |
| **Cross-reference** | Compare information across multiple sources for consistency |
| **Database screening** | OFAC SDN list, SEC enforcement actions, state registries |

### 3.5 Timing

- Identity verification must be completed **before** subscription acceptance
- If verification cannot be completed within 30 days, the subscription must be rejected
- If discrepancies are identified, Enhanced Due Diligence (Section 6) applies

## 4. Accredited Investor Verification

### 4.1 Rule 506(b) / 506(c) Requirements

The Company's Reg D offering relies on Rule 506(b) or 506(c) [Pending counsel determination] of Regulation D. If Rule 506(c) is elected, the Company must take **reasonable steps to verify** that each purchaser is an accredited investor, and general solicitation is permitted but verification is mandatory. If Rule 506(b) is elected, the Company must have a reasonable belief that each purchaser is an accredited investor, and general solicitation is not permitted.

### 4.2 Accepted Verification Methods

The SEC has identified the following non-exclusive safe harbor methods:

**Income-Based Verification (Rule 501(a)(6)):**

| Method | Documentation Required |
|--------|----------------------|
| Tax returns | IRS forms (W-2, 1040, K-1) for the two most recent years showing income exceeding $200,000 (individual) or $300,000 (joint with spouse) |
| Third-party verification | Written confirmation from CPA, attorney, registered broker-dealer, or SEC-registered investment adviser |

**Net Worth-Based Verification (Rule 501(a)(5)):**

| Method | Documentation Required |
|--------|----------------------|
| Asset documentation | Recent bank statements, brokerage statements, appraisals, and tax assessments |
| Liability documentation | Credit report (not more than 3 months old) |
| Third-party verification | Written confirmation from CPA, attorney, registered broker-dealer, or SEC-registered investment adviser |

**Entity Verification (Rule 501(a)(1), (2), (3), (7), (8)):**

| Entity Type | Documentation Required |
|-------------|----------------------|
| Bank, insurance company, registered investment company | Evidence of registration/charter |
| Employee benefit plan (ERISA) | Plan documents and assets > $5M |
| Trust with assets > $5M | Trust agreement, asset statements |
| Entity in which all equity owners are accredited | Verification of each owner |
| Entity with total assets > $5M | Financial statements |

**Prior Verification:**

| Method | Requirements |
|--------|-------------|
| Existing investor re-verification | If previously verified within 5 years and self-certifies no material change, issuer may rely on prior verification |

### 4.3 Verification Process

1. **Subscription submission** -- Investor completes subscription agreement and selects verification method
2. **Documentation collection** -- Company or broker-dealer collects verification documents
3. **Review** -- Compliance Officer reviews documentation for completeness and authenticity
4. **Determination** -- Written determination of accredited investor status
5. **Acceptance or rejection** -- Subscription accepted or returned with explanation
6. **Record retention** -- All verification documents maintained per Section 9

### 4.4 Broker-Dealer Coordination

[To be designated] broker-dealer, as the Company's registered broker-dealer for the offering, will conduct its own AML/KYC and suitability review. For investors onboarded through the broker-dealer platform:

- Broker-dealer performs primary AML/KYC screening
- Broker-dealer verifies accredited investor status for Reg D subscriptions
- Broker-dealer provides the Company with confirmation of completed verification
- The Company retains the broker-dealer's confirmation in its compliance files
- The Company maintains the right to conduct supplemental verification

## 5. Know Your Customer Procedures

### 5.1 Risk-Based Approach

The Company assigns a risk rating to each investor based on:

| Risk Factor | Lower Risk | Higher Risk |
|-------------|-----------|-------------|
| **Geography** | US domestic investor | Foreign investor, high-risk jurisdiction |
| **Investment amount** | At or near minimum investment | Significantly above minimum |
| **Entity type** | Individual, US trust | Foreign entity, shell company, nominee structure |
| **Source of funds** | Employment, investment returns | Business income from high-risk industry, inheritance (unverified) |
| **Investor profile** | Consistent with stated income/net worth | Inconsistent financial profile |
| **Political exposure** | No political exposure | Politically Exposed Person (PEP) or close associate |
| **Industry connection** | No connection to psychedelic/wellness industry | Active participant in psychedelic therapy or alternative medicine sectors (additional diligence on source of funds) |

### 5.2 Standard Due Diligence (All Investors)

1. Collect identification information (Section 3)
2. Verify identity (Section 3.4)
3. Screen against OFAC SDN List (Section 8)
4. Verify accredited investor status (Section 4)
5. Review subscription documents for completeness and consistency
6. Document source of funds (self-certification at minimum)
7. Retain records per Section 9

### 5.3 Source of Funds

All investors must provide a source of funds declaration. Acceptable sources include:

- Employment income / salary
- Business income / self-employment
- Investment returns / capital gains
- Retirement distributions
- Inheritance (with documentation)
- Sale of real property (with documentation)
- Gift (with documentation and donor identification)

## 6. Enhanced Due Diligence

### 6.1 Triggers for Enhanced Due Diligence

Enhanced due diligence is required when:

- Investor is rated as higher risk under Section 5.1
- Investor is a Politically Exposed Person (PEP) or close associate
- Investor is located in or connected to a jurisdiction identified by FATF as high-risk
- Investment amount is inconsistent with investor's stated financial profile
- Investor uses complex entity structures with no apparent business purpose
- Source of funds cannot be readily verified
- Any red flag from Section 7 is identified

### 6.2 Enhanced Due Diligence Procedures

1. **Senior review** -- Subscription reviewed by CEO or designee in addition to Compliance Officer
2. **Additional documentation** -- Request additional source of funds/wealth documentation
3. **Third-party database screening** -- Run enhanced searches through WorldCheck, Dow Jones Risk & Compliance, or equivalent
4. **Entity transparency** -- For entity investors, identify ultimate beneficial owners through all layers of ownership
5. **PEP screening** -- Screen against PEP databases for politically exposed persons
6. **Legal review** -- If warranted, engage [To be designated] legal counsel for legal analysis
7. **Written determination** -- Document the basis for acceptance or rejection of the subscription

### 6.3 Prohibited Investors

The Company will not accept subscriptions from:

- Any person or entity on the OFAC Specially Designated Nationals (SDN) List
- Any person or entity on the OFAC Consolidated Sanctions List
- Any person or entity subject to sectoral sanctions
- Any person or entity located in, or controlled by persons located in, comprehensively sanctioned jurisdictions (currently: Cuba, Iran, North Korea, Syria, Crimea/Donetsk/Luhansk regions)
- Any person or entity that the Company reasonably believes is engaged in money laundering, terrorist financing, or other illicit financial activity

## 7. Suspicious Activity Identification

### 7.1 Red Flags

The following indicators may suggest suspicious activity:

**Identity Red Flags:**
- Reluctance to provide required identification
- Identification documents that appear altered or fraudulent
- Information provided is inconsistent across documents
- Use of multiple identities or aliases
- Communication only through intermediaries or agents

**Financial Red Flags:**
- Investment amount inconsistent with investor's financial profile
- Source of funds from high-risk jurisdictions
- Wire transfers from unrelated third parties
- Request to structure transactions to avoid reporting thresholds
- Frequent changes to subscription amounts without explanation
- Request for unusual speed in closing

**Entity Red Flags:**
- Complex multi-layered entity structures with no clear business purpose
- Bearer shares or nominee shareholders
- Entity recently formed in secrecy jurisdiction immediately prior to investment
- Reluctance to identify beneficial owners
- PO Box as only address for an operating entity

**Medical Wellness / Psychedelic Therapy Industry Red Flags:**
- Investors claiming connections to unregulated psychedelic operations
- Source of funds tied to jurisdictions with known narcotics trafficking activity
- Unusually large cash-equivalent investments from individuals with stated involvement in alternative medicine with no verifiable business history

### 7.2 Reporting Procedures

If suspicious activity is identified:

1. **Document** the suspicious activity or red flags observed
2. **Do not alert** the investor or suspected party ("tipping off" prohibition)
3. **Report immediately** to the Compliance Officer
4. **Compliance Officer review** within 24 hours
5. **Escalation** to CEO and legal counsel as warranted
6. **SAR filing** with FinCEN if required (within 30 days of detection)
7. **Subscription disposition** -- reject, hold, or accept with enhanced monitoring
8. **Retain all records** related to the suspicious activity determination

## 8. OFAC Screening

### 8.1 Screening Requirements

All prospective investors must be screened against the following OFAC lists before subscription acceptance:

| List | Source | Frequency |
|------|--------|-----------|
| **Specially Designated Nationals (SDN) List** | OFAC | Every new subscription |
| **Consolidated Sanctions List** | OFAC | Every new subscription |
| **Sectoral Sanctions Identifications (SSI) List** | OFAC | Every new subscription |
| **Non-SDN Menu-Based Sanctions (NS-MBS) List** | OFAC | Risk-based |

### 8.2 Screening Process

1. **Name screening** -- Screen investor name (and all known aliases) against OFAC lists
2. **Entity screening** -- For entity investors, screen entity name, all beneficial owners, and control persons
3. **Address screening** -- Verify investor address is not in a comprehensively sanctioned jurisdiction
4. **Potential match review** -- If a potential match is identified:
   - Compare all available identifying information (name, DOB, address, citizenship)
   - Determine if the match is a true positive or false positive
   - If true positive: reject subscription and file reports as required
   - If false positive: document the basis for the determination
5. **Record results** of all screenings

### 8.3 Ongoing Screening

- Re-screen all existing investors against updated OFAC lists **monthly**
- If an existing investor becomes a match, immediately:
  - Freeze the investor's account
  - Report to OFAC within 10 business days
  - Engage legal counsel
  - Do not release funds or process distributions without OFAC guidance
