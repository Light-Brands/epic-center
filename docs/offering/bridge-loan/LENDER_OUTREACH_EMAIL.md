## Response Handling Playbook

### If lender says "send me more":
Attach the term sheet gist link (secret): https://gist.github.com/autod3v/1131e86d4ec45c9b8074cf66e013fd60
Offer to schedule a call. Request their standard NDA form, or offer yours.

### If lender asks for an appraisal:
Acknowledge that a formal Mexican appraisal ("avalúo comercial") will be commissioned during diligence at sponsor cost. The market value reference in the package is based on the public Christie's listing plus the Casa Luna comparable. Offer to share the comps memo directly.

### If lender flags the 84% LTV as high:
Reframe around two points. First, the $11.9M market anchor is independently validated (not just our asking number); Casa Luna is the second anchor. Second, the $25M+ replacement-cost backstop sits beneath the market value. At $10M, the loan is 40% of prior construction capital, not 84% of a thin market number.

### If lender counters at $8M at 9% instead of $10M at 9%:
That is the prior framing and still executable. The $8M version sits at 67% LTV against the same $11.9M market anchor, which is well inside normal hard money bridge tolerance. Worth accepting if it closes fast with the right lender.

### If lender counters at $10M at 11-12% instead of $10M at 9%:
Acknowledge that a rate bump is a standard response to the 84% LTV stretch. Hold 9% by pointing to the short duration (36 months), first-lien position, no competing debt, open prepayment, and the replacement-cost cushion. If a concession is needed, prefer adding a 1% commitment fee over moving the headline rate.

### If lender asks about recourse or guarantees:
Optional corporate or personal guarantees are negotiable and subject to lender preference. Not automatic in the current package, but available as a structural accommodation.

### If lender questions the 14-day timeline:
Four facts make 14 days executable:
1. **Sponsor-side due diligence on the Property is complete.** Title, trust structure, permits, zoning, and physical condition have been verified directly with the current owner. There are no open property-side issues to work through.
2. The Property is already held in an existing Mexican real estate trust. We are assigning the fideicomisario beneficial interest at the existing trustee bank, not forming a new Fideicomiso. This removes 3-4 weeks of trust formation and SRE permitting from the critical path.
3. US and Mexican counsel are engaged and prepared to draft definitive documents in parallel from day one of LOI.
4. The data room is already populated and ready to deliver under NDA on day zero.
The 14-day timeline requires a lender with authority to commit without a multi-week credit committee cycle. That typically means a private bridge fund, family office, or relationship lender with existing cross-border Mexican real estate capability. Institutional HMLs that require a formal credit committee process are not the right fit for this timeline.
