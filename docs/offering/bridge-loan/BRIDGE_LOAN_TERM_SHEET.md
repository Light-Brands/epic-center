# PART I: LOAN TERM SHEET

### 1. Parties

| Role | Party |
|---|---|
| **Borrower** | Existing Mexican Real Estate Trust (Fideicomiso) currently holding title to the Property, via assignment of the fideicomisario beneficial interest to the sponsor at closing. Title remains with the existing Fideicomiso; only the beneficial interest transfers. |
| **Fiduciary Institution** | Existing Mexican bank trustee (Fiduciario) holding the Fideicomiso of record |
| **Incoming Fideicomisario (Beneficiary)** | Sponsor operating entity acquiring the beneficial interest at closing |
| **Lender** | [Lender Vehicle TBD] |
| **Collateral Agent** | [To be appointed by Lender] |

### 2. Loan Amount

**USD $10,000,000** (Ten Million United States Dollars), funded in a single drawdown at closing.

### 3. Interest Rate

**9.00% per annum**, simple interest, calculated on a 30/360 day basis on the outstanding principal balance.

### 4. Term

**36 months** from the Closing Date (the "Maturity Date"). No automatic extension. Extension by mutual written agreement only, subject to renegotiation of economic terms.

### 5. Payment Schedule

| Payment Type | Frequency | Amount |
|---|---|---|
| Interest | Quarterly in arrears | USD $225,000 per quarter |
| Principal | Bullet at Maturity | USD $10,000,000 |
| Total Interest Over Term | — | USD $2,700,000 |
| Total Cash to Lender | — | USD $12,700,000 |

First interest payment due ninety (90) days after the Closing Date. Subsequent payments due every ninety (90) days thereafter.

### 6. Prepayment

**Open prepayment:** Voluntary prepayment permitted in whole or in part at any time, without penalty, premium, or makewhole. No lockout period.

**Notice:** Thirty (30) days' prior written notice to Lender of any prepayment, or such shorter notice as the parties mutually agree.

**Application:** Partial prepayments shall be applied first to accrued but unpaid interest, then to principal. Interest accrues only through the date of prepayment.

### 7. Use of Proceeds

Proceeds shall be applied exclusively to:

- Acquisition of the Property (Calle Perico Lts 79-80, Av Las Palmas, Akumal, Tulum, Q.R. 77776, Mexico)
- Capital expenditures and operational buildout of the Transformational Epicenter project
- Reasonable and documented transaction costs (capped at 2% of principal)

No proceeds may be distributed to equity holders, affiliates, or used to repay subordinated debt without Lender consent.

### 8. Collateral and Security

**Primary Collateral:**
- First-priority lien on the Mexican real estate trust (fideicomiso) holding fee simple title to the Property, including all improvements, fixtures, and appurtenant rights.

**Additional Security:**
- Pledge of beneficial rights under the Fideicomiso (fideicomisario beneficial interest)
- Assignment of all insurance policies and proceeds relating to the Property
- Assignment of material construction contracts, architectural plans, and permits
- Control agreement over all operating accounts holding Project cash flows

**Market Value:** USD $11,900,000 (based on current public listing of the Property as "Rancho Paraiso Oasis" on Christie's International Real Estate, listing ID 047661).

**Construction Capital Deployed:** Over USD $25,000,000 of prior-owner capital deployed into land, improvements, and infrastructure (9 lots, 45,000 sqm, main house, guest house, spa, gym, cenote pool, cavern lounge, walls, staff infrastructure). Retained as replacement-cost reference, not as collateral value.

**Loan-to-Value Ratio:** 84% (against market value).

### 9. Representations and Warranties

Customary representations of a senior secured real estate bridge loan, including without limitation:

- Due organization, good standing, power and authority
- No conflict with organizational documents, material contracts, or applicable law
- No pending or threatened litigation material to Borrower or the Property
- Valid and enforceable title to the Property through the fideicomiso
- No undisclosed liens, encumbrances, or competing claims
- Full disclosure of material environmental, permitting, and regulatory matters
- Compliance with all Mexican tax, land use, and foreign investment regulations
- No bankruptcy, insolvency, or dissolution events pending

### 10. Affirmative Covenants

Borrower shall, at a minimum:

- Maintain existence, permits, and licenses required for the Property
- Deliver quarterly financial statements and semi-annual project reports
- Maintain property insurance at full replacement value, naming Lender as loss payee
- Pay all taxes, assessments, and trust fees when due
- Preserve the Property in good condition and continue approved development work
- Provide Lender with site inspection rights on reasonable notice
- Notify Lender promptly of any Event of Default or material adverse development

### 11. Negative Covenants

Without Lender's prior written consent, Borrower shall not:

- Incur additional indebtedness ranking pari passu or senior to the Loan
- Grant liens on the Collateral other than Permitted Liens
- Sell, transfer, or further encumber the Property or any material portion
- Make distributions to equity holders or affiliates
- Enter into material contracts outside the ordinary course of business
- Change the use of the Property or the scope of the Project
- Dissolve, merge, or undergo a change of control

### 12. Events of Default

The occurrence of any of the following shall constitute an Event of Default:

1. **Payment default:** Failure to pay interest within five (5) business days of due date, or principal on the Maturity Date
2. **Covenant breach:** Breach of any affirmative or negative covenant, uncured for thirty (30) days after notice
3. **Representation default:** Any representation or warranty proves materially false when made
4. **Cross-default:** Default on any other indebtedness of Borrower in excess of $250,000
5. **Insolvency:** Bankruptcy, receivership, assignment for the benefit of creditors, or similar event
6. **Lien impairment:** Any challenge to or impairment of Lender's first-priority lien
7. **Material adverse change:** A material adverse change in Borrower's ability to perform, as reasonably determined by Lender
8. **Judgment default:** Unsatisfied judgment in excess of $500,000 outstanding for more than thirty (30) days
9. **Permit revocation:** Revocation or suspension of any material permit required for the Project
10. **Abandonment:** Cessation of development work for more than sixty (60) consecutive days

### 13. Remedies Upon Default

Upon any Event of Default, Lender shall have the right, in its sole discretion, to:

- **Accelerate** all outstanding principal and accrued interest, immediately due and payable
- **Foreclose** on the Collateral through the fideicomiso enforcement mechanism under Mexican law
- **Step in** and assume operational control of the Project, including replacement of management
- **Appoint a receiver** over the Property and operating accounts
- **Exercise** all rights under the pledged fideicomisario beneficial interest, including substitution of the beneficiary
- **Set off** any amounts held in controlled accounts against outstanding obligations
- **Pursue** any other remedies available at law or in equity

Default interest shall accrue at the Interest Rate plus 4.00% per annum from the date of the Event of Default.

### 14. Cross-Border Enforcement

**Governing Law:** New York law governs the Loan Agreement, the Note, and all ancillary US-law documents. Mexican law governs the fideicomiso, the lien recording, and Mexican enforcement proceedings.

**Jurisdiction:** Non-exclusive submission to New York State and US Federal courts for the Loan Agreement. Exclusive jurisdiction of Mexican courts for fideicomiso enforcement.

**Waiver of Sovereign Immunity:** Borrower waives any claim of sovereign immunity.

**Foreign Judgment Recognition:** Borrower agrees that US judgments shall be recognized and enforceable in Mexico under applicable treaty and comity principles.

### 15. Conditions Precedent to Closing

- Satisfactory completion of legal, title, environmental, and regulatory due diligence
- Execution of definitive Loan Agreement and all security documents
- Recording of the first-priority lien on the fideicomiso with the Mexican Public Registry
- Delivery of title insurance acceptable to Lender
- Delivery of opinions of US and Mexican counsel
- Delivery of executed guarantees (if any)
- Completion of KYC, AML, and sanctions screening on Borrower and affiliates
- Payment of Lender's legal and transaction costs
- No material adverse change between signing and closing

### 16. Fees and Expenses

- **Commitment Fee:** None
- **Legal and Diligence Costs:** Borrower bears all reasonable and documented legal, title, diligence, and recording costs incurred by Lender
- **Exit Fee:** None

### 17. Confidentiality

Both parties agree to maintain the confidentiality of this term sheet and all related negotiations, except as required by law, counsel, or bona fide prospective co-lenders under customary NDA protection.

### 18. Exclusivity

Upon execution of this term sheet, Borrower agrees to a twenty-one (21) day exclusivity period during which Borrower will not solicit, negotiate, or accept competing financing proposals for the Property. The 21-day window is sized to cover the targeted 14-day close with a seven-day cushion for final closing mechanics.

### 19. Binding Effect

This term sheet is **non-binding** other than Sections 16 (Fees and Expenses), 17 (Confidentiality), and 18 (Exclusivity), which are binding upon execution. Definitive documentation supersedes this term sheet in all other respects.

# PART IV: COMMERCIAL SUMMARY (One-Page Pitch)

**Transformational Epicenter: Senior Secured Bridge Loan**

**$10M at 9% for 36 months. Interest-only, quarterly. Bullet at maturity. No commitment fee. Open prepayment at any time. Borrower is a Mexican real estate trust (Fideicomiso) holding direct title to the Property.**

A senior secured bridge loan to fund the acquisition and buildout of a transformational wellness and hospitality property in Akumal, Quintana Roo, Mexico. The Property sits on 45,000 square meters across nine lots and is currently listed on Christie's International Real Estate as "Rancho Paraiso Oasis" at USD $11,900,000. Over $25 million of prior construction capital has been deployed into land, improvements, and infrastructure, embedded in the asset as a replacement-cost backstop beneath the market value.

**The Structure**
- **Borrower:** Existing Mexican real estate trust (Fideicomiso) already holding title to the Property. Sponsor acquires the fideicomisario beneficial interest at closing via assignment at the existing trustee bank. No new Fideicomiso formation required, no SRE permitting delay, no US or Delaware entity in the structure.
- **Collateral:** First-priority lien on the Fideicomiso's direct title to the Property, plus pledge of the fideicomisario beneficial interest, insurance assignment, and account control agreement.
- **Governing law:** Mexican law for the Fideicomiso, lien, and enforcement. Loan Agreement and Note governed by New York law for international lender comfort.

**For the Lender**
- $225,000 quarterly interest payments
- Full $10 million principal returned at month 36
- $12.7 million total cash out (27% gross return)
- First-priority lien on the Fideicomiso's direct title to the Property
- 84% loan-to-value against the $11.9M Christie's listing, with $25M+ of prior construction capital underneath as a replacement-cost backstop
- Senior position, step-in rights, clean Mexican enforcement pathway
- No commitment fee. Open prepayment at any time without penalty.

**For the Sponsor**
- Non-dilutive capital to complete buildout
- Simple single-jurisdiction structure with no US entity coordination
- Short duration (36 months) matched to the buildout to stabilization timeline
- Clean debt structure that does not complicate future equity capital
- Takeout at maturity through refinancing or sale

**Timeline:** Targeting closing within **14 days** of signed LOI. The deal structure uses assignment of the existing fideicomisario beneficial interest rather than new Fideicomiso formation, which removes the multi-week trust formation and SRE permitting steps and makes a 14-day close executable. Path-to-close detail available in the lender outreach package.
